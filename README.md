# Bright-Motors-Sales-Analysis
Data analysis of Bright Motors Sales

## Problem statement
- Bright Motors is a car sales company, that specialises in selling various car makes and models across different states.
- The new Head of Sales wants to expand the dealership network, improve sales performance and optimize inventory. 

## Aim
The aim of this project is to extract insights from the historical data and use them to help guide future sales and marketing strategies.

## Summary of results
- Total revenue of R7.61B from 558,811 units sold (2012–2015), with R7.0B generated in 2015 alone.
- Q1 dominates with 62% of revenue, while February (R2.2B) and January (R1.9B) are peak months.
- Ford leads all makes (R1.4B); F-150 is the top model (R272.7M) and dominates more than 5 states.
- FL, CA, PA and TX are the top-performing states, together they generate over R3.7B.
- 83.5% of sales fall into Low Margin tier, a major profitability concern that needs to be addressed.
- Newer cars manugactured between 2012 and 2015 have average selling prices (up to R50.9K), with lower mileage (approximately 24K).

## Recommendations
### Expansion:
- Use high-performing state-make/model combos to guide expansion when opening new dealerships.
- Focus on high-performing states and high performing make and model mixes, ensuring consistent stock availability to top sellers. 
- Reassess operations in underperforming states (relocation, downsizing, or digital-only presence).
### Sales Performance:
- Expand dealership presence during high-demand seasons (Quarter 1) to maximize on early-year demand. 
- Fix weak quarters (Quarters 3 and 4) by introducing low-season promotions such as discounts, financing deals, or trade-in incentives to improve yearly revenue distribution.
### Inventory Optimization:
- Align stock with mileage, age, and region-specific best selling makes and models. 
- Use a segment specific strategy such that in premium dealerships you source low-mileage newer cars, to increase average selling price and improve profitability. And in Budget dealerships you source high mileage older cars.
### Profitability:
- Move away from heavy dependence on low-margin sales, by changing pricing and product mix strategy toward medium/high-margin sales. 
- Negotiate supplier partnerships with top make/model to increase average selling price and improve margins.
- Replicate peak-period sales strategies (campaigns, promotions, dealer incentives), used around early 2015 to sustain high revenue periods.
